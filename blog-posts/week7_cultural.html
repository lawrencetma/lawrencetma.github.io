Measuring Values

When looking at that giant list of values, I repeated to myself, happiest, proudest, most satisfied. Although I could probably make a case for any of them I decided I would choose one value for each of those feelings.

Excitement

When I think of the happiest time in my life so far I think of Excitement. When my fiance said yes after I asked her to marry me, when the Giants won the World Series in 2010 and 2012 or even something as small as coming home after a long day to my dog happily jumping on me and trying to lick me.

All these things embody Excitement in some way or another and I think a lot of the happiest moments of my life are also exciting moments.

Although I know that I enjoy excitement in my life, often it's difficult to break out of the daily monotony and try something new, and try to obtain that excitement on my own. I would say that on a scale of 1-5 I am a 3 or a 4 in living up to that value.

Accomplishment

Accomplishment is a great feeling and something that emobidies what you are most proud of. You had a difficult goal and you accomplished that goal through hard work.

When I think of things such as graduating from college or law school, passing the bar, or even completing last week's solo challenge, accomplishment is a great feeling at the end of a tough road.

One thing I loved from early on in phase 0 was the motivation technique where you would segment your work into little accomplishments instead of focusing only on the large accomplishment.

Instead of being discouraged that it would take so much work to accomplish a large goal, accomplishing small goals that contribute to that large goal make it a lot easier to motivate yourself to keep going.

I love that feeling of accomplishment and I try to live up to it every day by celebrating my small accomplishments as much as I can. I feel I'm at a 4 on a scale of 1-5.

Friendships

I know I've been truly blessed in my life not only to have many close friends surrounding me who love and support me, but also that I can say that my family members are also some of my closest friends. I'm very satisfied with my friendships and the relationships I've fostered with people in my life.

As far as friendships go I feel like there are times where I could have done more for my friends and been for them more often but I strive to be a great friend whenever possible. I would say that I live up to my friendships value fairly well and would give myself a 4 out of 5 on that scale.

Advice

Last time I remember giving advice was probably a few weeks ago when I told my parents that even though they are getting older that it doesn't mean they can't improve themselves a little every day.

There are some things physically that will decline regardless of what you do, but the mind can be constantly improved and I encouraged my parents, my mom in particular to read more, and write more.

Values and Stereotype Threat

When I think of my values in conjunction with stereotype threats or anything that might hamper my motivation or performance actually I think as long as I go back to keeping it simple and working on small accomplishments I'll be fine.

I always try to remind myself that no one learns something immediately, that it takes time, practice and patience. THere may be so much information that sometimes I might forget something I had learned previously, but it's much easier to re-learn something through review.

There are enough pressures in life, I try my very best to not be my own worst enemy and put undue pressure on myself to succeed. Hopefully this is a mindset I can keep throughout my time at DBC and beyond.
